NI Imports via Windsor Framework

No Customs Duty • No VAT on Import
The Windsor Framework means goods moving from Great Britain to Northern Ireland (and then to you in ROI) may qualify for zero customs and zero VAT on import — saving €8,000–€12,000 vs direct GB imports.

A car registered in Northern Ireland is treated as an EU/UK internal movement. Once imported to ROI, you only pay VRT (Vehicle Registration Tax) — not customs duty (0%) or import VAT (0% on NI-sourced cars).

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Find the car on usedcarsni.com — filter by Hybrid, automatic, <70k km, 2021+.
  2. Verify NI ownership history — car must have been registered in NI (not just sitting there). Check service history, previous MOTs, and that the seller is an NI-based dealer or owner.
  3. View/Test drive — travel to NI (Belfast is ~2h from Dublin). Most dealers allow test drives with proof of insurance.
  4. Purchase & get documents: Invoice, V5C (logbook), MOT cert, proof of NI registration history.
  5. Arrange transport — drive it back or use a transporter (~€150–€300 from Belfast to Dublin).
  6. Pay VRT — use Revenue's VRT calculator to estimate. For a €26k hybrid, expect ~€3k–€6k VRT depending on CO₂. Book an appointment at your local NCTS centre.
  7. Register in ROI — after VRT paid, you get an Irish registration number. Update insurance with the new reg.
  8. Total saving: Typically €2,000–€5,000 cheaper than identical ROI-listed car (sometimes more for higher-value vehicles).

⚠ Beware of "NI Delivery" scams — Some GB sellers offer "free NI delivery" to bypass customs. Only buy from NI-registered dealers with physical premises. The car must have NI service/MOT history to qualify for Windsor Framework benefits. If in doubt, ask for proof of NI registration before the sale.
